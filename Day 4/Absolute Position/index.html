Absolutely positioned elements do not affect the layout of other elements. They can overlap or be overlapped by other elements.

In summary:

  • Relative positioning is useful for creating subtle adjustments to an element's position within its normal flow
  • Absolute positioning is ideal for creating elements that are positioned precisely relative to a specific container or the entire page, and that do not affect the layout of other elements.

    • Note: Most of the time, absolutely positioned elements with height and width properties set up to auto are sized to fit their contents. However, non-replaced and absolutely positioned elements can fill the available vertical space when specified with both top and bottom properties leaving height.
